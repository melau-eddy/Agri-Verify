Usage Restrictions

Maintain 660ft (200m) isolation distance from organic corn fields to prevent cross-pollination.

Plant 20% refuge area with non-Bt corn varieties to prevent pest resistance development.

Follow Insect Resistance Management (IRM) plan as specified in product documentation.

Report any unexpected adverse effects or performance issues to BioTech Agri Solutions and USDA.

Planting Guidelines

1
Site Selection & Preparation

  • Choose well-drained soil with pH between 5.8 and 7.0 for optimal growth.
  • Prepare seedbed by removing debris and breaking up soil clumps to ensure good seed-to-soil contact.
  • Apply pre-plant fertilizer according to soil test recommendations, typically 60-80 lbs/acre of nitrogen.
  • Ensure soil temperature is at least 50°F (10°C) at planting depth for proper germination.

2
Planting Technique

  • Plant at a depth of 1.5-2 inches (3.8-5 cm) in moist soil conditions.
  • Maintain row spacing of 30-36 inches (76-91 cm) and plant spacing of 6-8 inches (15-20 cm).
  • Target a final plant population of 28,000-32,000 plants per acre for optimal yield.
  • Plant refuge areas as required (20% of total corn acreage) with non-Bt corn varieties.

3
Crop Management

  • Apply nitrogen fertilizer in split applications: 1/3 at planting and 2/3 at V6-V8 growth stage.
  • Monitor for secondary pests not controlled by the built-in resistance traits.
  • Implement integrated weed management practices to prevent herbicide resistance.
  • While drought-tolerant, provide supplemental irrigation during extended drought if available, especially during critical growth stages.

4
Harvesting & Storage

  • Harvest when grain moisture content is between 23-25% for optimal quality.
  • Dry harvested grain to 15% moisture content for short-term storage or 13% for long-term storage.
  • Store in clean, dry facilities with proper aeration and pest management.
  • Monitor stored grain regularly for temperature, moisture, and pest activity.

Best Practices for Drought Conditions

Early Season Management

  • Implement conservation tillage to preserve soil moisture.
  • Apply mulch to reduce evaporation from soil surface.
  • Control early-season weeds that compete for moisture.

Mid-Season Management

  • Prioritize irrigation during critical pollination period if available.
  • Apply foliar nutrients to support plant health during stress.
  • Monitor for increased pest pressure during drought conditions.
